The expansion and modernization of the São Paulo Metro reflect the urgency of a Brazil on the move, seeking efficiency and sustainability in its transportation system.

<p>Every great city reveals its intelligence by how it moves people. And in São Paulo, this intelligence is in full expansion. With a record budget of R$5.4 billion for 2026, the largest in history, the São Paulo Metro not only improves its system but also sets an efficiency standard for all of Brazil, as pointed out by <a href="https://www.ocafezinho.com/2026/06/08/expansao-do-metro-de-sao-paulo-reflete-necessidade-de-modernizacao-no-transporte-urbano-brasileiro/" target="_blank">O Cafezinho</a>.</p>
<p>The core of the project is the expansion of Line 2-Green, which will gain 13.8 km of new tracks and 13 new stations, divided into two phases. The first phase, with 55% completion, already features eight new stations; the second brings five more. This expansion is not merely an extension of lines; it is an investment in urban mobility and local economic development, facilitating access to jobs and essential services, as detailed by <a href="https://www.ocafezinho.com/2026/06/08/expansao-do-metro-de-sao-paulo-reflete-necessidade-de-modernizacao-no-transporte-urbano-brasileiro/" target="_blank">O Cafezinho</a>.</p>
<p>São Paulo also adopts the CBTC (Communication-Based Train Control) system, a train control technology based on continuous communication. It allows shorter intervals and greater frequency, which means more trains per hour and higher transport capacity. Without the need for new civil works, the city is already in line with the efficiency of metro systems in Singapore and London, as highlighted by <a href="https://www.ocafezinho.com/2026/06/08/expansao-do-metro-de-sao-paulo-reflete-necessidade-de-modernizacao-no-transporte-urbano-brasileiro/" target="_blank">O Cafezinho</a>.</p>
<p>The adoption of platform doors, known as PSDs (Platform Screen Doors), is another advancement. They eliminate accident risks, reduce energy consumption, and speed up boarding and alighting. The purchase of 63 new trains for lines 1-Blue, 2-Green, 3-Red, and 15-Silver completes the rolling stock renewal, increasing system reliability and reducing maintenance costs, according to <a href="https://oglobo.globo.com/opiniao/editorial/coluna/2026/07/expansao-do-metro-paulistano-serve-de-exemplo-para-todo-o-brasil.ghtml" target="_blank">Globo</a>.</p>
<p>Larger investments are also being made in Line 15-Silver, with R$1.03 billion, allowing for the delivery of 19 new trains, of which 15 have already been delivered. And Line 17-Gold, with R$836.3 million budgeted to connect Congonhas Airport to Morumbi Station, symbolizes a delivery promised since the 2014 World Cup, as reported by <a href="https://www.ocafezinho.com/2026/06/08/expansao-do-metro-de-sao-paulo-reflete-necessidade-de-modernizacao-no-transporte-urbano-brasileiro/" target="_blank">O Cafezinho</a>.</p>
<p>The 2025 performance, with R$4.51 billion invested in works, shows the seriousness of the commitment. The total package of ongoing works in the state metro system amounts to R$33 billion, with future lines such as 19-Celeste, 20-Rosa, and 22-Marrom that will connect São Paulo to neighboring cities, according to <a href="https://www.ocafezinho.com/2026/06/08/expansao-do-metro-de-sao-paulo-reflete-necessidade-de-modernizacao-no-transporte-urbano-brasileiro/" target="_blank">O Cafezinho</a>.</p>
<p>São Paulo, historically underinvested in mobility infrastructure, now chooses the path of rail. This not only meets immediate urban mobility needs but also positions the city as a model of modernization for other Brazilian cities, combining robust investments, cutting-edge technology, and strategic planning, as highlighted by <a href="https://www.ocafezinho.com/2026/06/08/expansao-do-metro-de-sao-paulo-reflete-necessidade-de-modernizacao-no-transporte-urbano-brasileiro/" target="_blank">O Cafezinho</a>.</p>
